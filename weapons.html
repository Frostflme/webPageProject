Splatoon 3 Weapons

Here, you'll get a basic rundown of weapons, and you'll be able to navigate to different pages to get more in-depth information on more specific weapons. First, we should talk about the common Splatoon termonology.

Common Termonology

Kits: A set of a sub weapon and a special weapon.

Classes: What category of weapons a specific main weapon falls into.

Someone's 'Main' (Not to be confused by main weapons): Someone's signature weapon; what someone plays often

Slayer: A weapon that's suited better to playing aggressive and splatting the opposing team.

Skirmisher: Skirmishers are most useful for controlling areas or forcing advantageous fights.

Support: A weapon that's suited better to hanging back and painting for special or playing the objective.

Anchor: A weapon that's suited better to staying at a distance and holding the team together.

(Tip: If any of this confuses you, try going to the original page for a more in-depth explanation!)

Main Weapons

Shooters: The most versatile class, if you pick the right shooter, it can do really whatever you want it to.

Blasters: Used to be considered a shooter sub-class, they have slow shots that are strong and explode in a specific radius.

Rollers: A beginner-friendly class, they rely on either being stealthy or applying pressure from a distance.

Brushes: A movement-oriented class, the brushes can move quickly across the map, and they excel at countering slow weapons.

Chargers: A pressuring class, chargers apply a crazy amount of pressure with their one-shots on a full charge*.

Sloshers: A wild-card class, they can hit over walls, but all of them work differently.

Splatlings: Another pressuring class, splatlings charge then fire many shots very quickly.

Dualies: A movement-oriented class, dualies can do dodge rolls and evade attacks from others.

Brellas: A defensive class, brellas have a shield to block incoming fire**, and their attacks have a shotgun-like spread.

Splatanas: A hyper-aggressive class, the splatanas can one-shot in melee, and they have longer ranged options too.

Stringers: My personal favorite, stringers can pressure opponents over walls, and they can one-shot if you hit all three pellets.

*not all chargers can one-shot on a full charge

**as of version 1.2.0, brella's sheilds don't work properly

Sub Weapons

Bombs: Being versatile options, bombs have a variety of uses from getting away to disrupting anchors.

Utility Subs: Often useful for a few specific purposes, they can be useful on the right weapons but aren't as versatile as bombs.

Special Weapons

Aggressive Specials: Special weapons that are often most useful to splat the opponents, often most useful on slayer weapons.

Supportive Specials: Supportive specials are used for protecting areas, controlling space, or otherwise helping the team.

Displacement Specials: Displacement specials are used to force people to move, distracting them or forcing them out of an area.
